# Reading Failure Types  
## A Canonical, Non-Tunable Set

This document defines a closed set of fourteen *reading failure types*.  
These are not interpretive disagreements, doctrinal positions, or textual errors.  
They are **structural ways in which judgment is prematurely completed while reading is still underway**.

This set is **normative** rather than empirical.  
It is not learned, optimized, extended, or revised by Owlume or any other system.

---

## What “Reading Failure” Means Here

A reading failure does **not** mean that a reader has misunderstood a text.

A reading failure occurs when **judgment is formed before reading has fully occurred**.

Reading is treated here as a temporal activity that necessarily involves:
- attention,
- exposure to tension,
- and suspension of closure.

When closure is reached too early—whether through explanation, moralization, reassurance, or positioning—reading collapses into something else: interpretation, evaluation, or comfort.

The failure is one of **timing**, not intelligence.

---

## Origin of the Set

These fourteen failure types do **not** originate from:
- biblical studies,
- theology,
- Owlume usage data,
- or model-driven analysis.

They are derived from the **structure of human judgment under uncertainty**, specifically:
- how people attempt to reduce cognitive load,
- escape unresolved tension,
- or restore certainty when meaning has not yet stabilized.

They apply to any high-tension text (scripture, literature, policy, testimony, AI output), not only to the Bible.

The Bible is used as a practice field because it amplifies these failures most clearly—not because it uniquely produces them.

---

## The Closed Set

The following fourteen reading failure types constitute a **closed classification**.
No additional types may be introduced.

Any newly observed reading difficulty must be:
- mapped to one of these types, or
- excluded as outside the scope of “reading failure” as defined here.

---

## The Fourteen Reading Failure Types

### RF01 · Mechanization  
Reducing narrative or symbolic text to a functional or explanatory mechanism before its meaning has been endured.

---

### RF02 · Position-Taking  
Occupying a stance of agreement, disagreement, or evaluation before reading has finished unfolding.

---

### RF03 · Premature Moralization  
Collapsing narrative tension into early judgments of good and bad, right and wrong.

---

### RF04 · Linguistic Drift Blindness  
Failing to notice that shifts in language are themselves actions that alter meaning, risk, or responsibility.

---

### RF05 · Responsibility Displacement  
Redirecting agency or consequence away from the reader or subject to preserve moral or emotional comfort.

---

### RF06 · Fairness Projection  
Imposing external standards of fairness or proportionality before the text has disclosed its own internal logic.

---

### RF07 · System Substitution  
Replacing personal position or responsibility with systems, structures, scale, or abstraction.

---

### RF08 · Promise Instrumentalization  
Using future resolution or assurance to neutralize present uncertainty or obligation.

---

### RF09 · Acceleration Substitution  
Attempting to bypass waiting, silence, or indeterminacy through premature action.

---

### RF10 · Spiritual (or Moral) Elitism  
Interpreting selection, success, or survival as evidence of superiority or moral rank.

---

### RF11 · Temporal Compression  
Eliminating duration, delay, or development by leaping directly from beginning to outcome.

---

### RF12 · Premature Consolation  
Deploying meaning, reassurance, or closure before loss, ambiguity, or harm has been fully faced.

---

### RF13 · Interpretive Substitution  
Replacing the act of reading with explanation, summary, or conceptual mastery.

---

### RF14 · Tension Relief Compulsion  
Treating unresolved tension as a defect rather than as a condition of meaning.

---

## Non-Negotiable Constraints

- This set is **closed**.
- These types are **non-tunable**.
- They are **constraints on systems**, not outputs of systems.
- No system may invent, merge, split, rank, or weight these types.

They exist to **limit what a system is allowed to do**, not to expand its capabilities.

---

## Relationship to Owlume

Owlume does not discover these failure types.

Owlume operates **under** them.

Its role is to:
- detect conditions where these failures are likely,
- intervene to slow judgment,
- and return responsibility to the reader.

If Owlume ceased to exist, this classification would remain valid.

If this classification were removed, Owlume would lose its grounding.

## Final Note

These failure types do not describe *what* a reader thinks.  
They describe **when a reader stops reading**.

That moment is the only thing this discipline governs.
